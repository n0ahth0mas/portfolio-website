The Uncanny Celebrity   
Exploring the Implications of Virtual Influencers on Instagram

Spring 2019 - Online Communication 

Project Description

I like to consider this two-part project as my first encounter with  transmedia storytelling. I stumbled across Miquela Sosa's Instagram page in the summer of 2018, when I was researching best practices for a brand's social media and instantly found myself entranced by everything about her and the mysterious company that created her, Brud. When prompted to create a 'bloggable' material about a prevalent trend online for my Online Communication class (University of Puget Sound, Spring 2019) I knew that Miquela Sosa was the answer. In this Vox-style video, I cover topics such as 'the glance,' 'the gaze,' 'the uncanny valley,' and more using language that anyone is able to understand.                   

This project was so well received by my peers and professor that I felt inspired to expand upon this research for my final project, a research prospectus which asked me to lay the groundwork for a theoretical study on digital influences utilizing peer reviewed research. If all requirements were met - as they were with this project - the paper would theoretically be IRB approvable. At the request of my professor, I submitted this paper to the PNW Communication Studies Undergraduate Research Conference, which unfortunately had to be cancelled. This paper examines digital influencers and asks questions about their appeal based on concepts such as 'the gaze,' and 'the uncanny valley,' and expands upon previous research about digital avatars in movies and video games.
